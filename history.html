Originally called Loidis...

Leeds was first recalled to exist by its mention in the Domesday Book, with a population of 200 and its spelling was: "Ledes". It began as an insignificant settlement during the period of the Norman Conquest, it then later evolved into one of the top three British cities, for the wollen industry during the Industrial Revolution in the 17th and 18th century, gained city status in 1893 and is now renouned for its festivals, meuseums and wildlife.
